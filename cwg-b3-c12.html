To The Point
What if denying your own greatness
is not modesty —
but a quiet denial of God?

Chapter 12 of Conversations with God — Book 3 opens with a disarming question: how does one stay aware of God? The answer is not withdrawal, not ritual — but expression. Become the source of what you seek. If you want love, become loving. If you want peace, become peaceful. If you want to know God, stop shrinking yourself.

From there the chapter moves through three territories: identity and self-denial, the nature of the soul, and love as freedom. These are not separate subjects — they are one argument, building toward a single recognition. The soul is not a small thing hidden inside a body. It is vast, stretching across time, animating many forms, incapable of being confined by any single biography. And love, when it is real, does not seek to contain. It seeks to free.

This production asks what those teachings look like when filtered through one human life — through faith tested under financial uncertainty, through identity shaped by a Hungarian literary memory of a boy who trusted God completely, through a marriage between two free souls who chose a shared unknown. Not as proof. As recognition.

Becoming Larger
  • Declare your greatness publicly
  • Be the source of what you seek
  • Soul larger than biography
  • Love as renewed daily choice
  • Trust without guaranteed outcome
  • Identity beyond survival
