To The Point
A civilization does not collapse when it runs out of resources —
it collapses when it can no longer distinguish reality
from the story it prefers.

This episode asks the question most civilizational analysis avoids: what if the systems we are trying to fix are not the problem, but the symptom? Inspired directly by Conversations with God — Book 3, Chapter 16, where denial, separation, and civilizational forgetting are examined as the root operating conditions of a society in decline, these productions translate that diagnosis into a contemporary context shaped by AI acceleration, collective consciousness research, and the widening gap between what humanity knows and how it acts. The central argument is not spiritual. It is structural. The hardware never fixes the software. And the software — the story a civilization believes about itself — always comes first.
