To The Point
The system is not broken.
It worked.
That is the problem.

For millennia, human civilization operated on a single inherited pattern: Do → Have → Be. You act, you acquire, you become. This was not a cultural mistake. It was nature itself — every species on the planet runs the same operating system. The predator hunts. The prey escapes. Energy moves through the chain. Humanity didn't invent this. We inherited it. Inspired by Conversations with God — Book 3, Chapter 19, where Highly Evolved Beings and the two foundational principles — We Are All One and There's Enough — are introduced, these productions explore what happens when AI begins to remove the survival pressure that made the old system necessary. The identity crisis this creates is not economic. It is existential. The real question is not which system wins. The real question is: who are you before survival defines you? And the answer — as the fisherman already knew — cannot be automated, simulated, or outsourced. It has to be lived.
