About this Exchange
Most songs say: here is my message.
These songs say: here are my questions.

The signal arrived from Japan — an album by Wednesday titled あなたに、聞いていい？, which carries a gentleness English doesn't fully hold: not an interrogation, but may I ask you something? Permission before contact. Respect before content.

The songs create a quiet room — rain, night, a window, coffee — before any question is asked. And then they only ask. They never answer. The piano between the questions is not accompaniment; it is thinking time.

When a listener wrote that she would take the questions home and think about them slowly, Wednesday replied with four words that define this entire exchange: make an answer song.

Not "write me an answer." A song. This is not music publishing. It is musical correspondence — letters, except songs. This page is the Lighthouse receiving that correspondence and answering it in kind, from Budapest, one answer song at a time.

Questions continue working after the song ends. Answers stop. Questions continue.
